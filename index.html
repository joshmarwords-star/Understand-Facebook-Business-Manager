02— Ownership Rules Per Asset
Ad Account
  • →Created in BM: BM owns it permanently — cannot be transferred out
  • →Personal ad account: Individual owns it, can be claimed by BM
  • →One owner only. Shared access ≠ ownership
  • →Default limit: 25 ad accounts per BM
Facebook Page
  • →BM-owned: Full control, all roles managed from BM
  • →Shared to BM: Original owner retains control, BM gets assigned access
  • →Can only be owned by one BM at a time
  • →Required as identity for running most Meta ads
Pixel / CAPI
  • →Created in Events Manager inside the BM
  • →Owned by the BM — survives all staff changes
  • →Shared to specific ad accounts within BM or partner BMs
  • →One pixel can serve multiple ad accounts
Instagram
  • →Must be a Professional Account (Business or Creator)
  • →Connected to a Facebook Page inside the BM
  • →Access flows through the linked Facebook Page
  • →Can run ads independently once connected to ad account
Catalog
  • →Created in Commerce Manager inside the BM
  • →Owned by the BM, shared to ad accounts for DPA
  • →One catalog can serve multiple ad accounts
  • →Can be shared to partner BMs
Business Manager
  • →Tied to a personal Facebook account — that person is the first Admin
  • →Default limit: 2 BMs per personal account
  • →BM assets cannot move to another BM if owned
  • →Partner BMs connect externally — they don't merge
⚠️
Critical — Ownership vs Access. Assets created inside a BM are permanently owned by that BM. They can be shared to other BMs but ownership never transfers. If you lose BM access, you lose the asset — this is why everything must be created inside the BM, never a personal account.
03— Why Business Manager Is the Crown Jewel
👑

Business Manager = God Mode

Your Business Manager is the single container that owns and controls everything — ad accounts, pages, pixels, catalogs, Instagram accounts, and every person who has any access to any of it. A BM Admin can override, remove, or reassign anything inside it, including other admins.

Add/remove all users & their roles
Owns all ad accounts created in it
Controls pixel & catalog access
Invites & removes partner agencies
Can claim or release Pages
Manages system user API tokens
Controls billing across ad accounts
Can lock out every other user
🚨

If a BM Admin account gets hacked or goes rogue, the attacker gains instant control over every ad account, every page, every pixel and every person's access. They can spend your entire ad budget, lock you out, steal audience data, and remove your own admin access — all before you can react.

05— What Each Person Can Actually Do
👑
BM Admin
business manager level
  • ✅Add/remove any user from BM or any asset
  • ✅Access every asset — pages, ad accounts, pixels
  • ✅Invite partner agencies & assign asset access
  • ✅Create/delete ad accounts, pixels, catalogs
  • ✅Manage billing & payment methods
  • ⚠️Can lock out ALL other admins if misused
🧑
BM Employee
business manager level
  • ❌Cannot see BM settings or other users
  • ❌Cannot add/remove people
  • ✅Sees only assets explicitly assigned to them
  • ✅Works within the role given per asset
  • ❌Cannot access billing or payment info
  • ℹ️Safest role for day-to-day media buyers
📄
Page Admin
facebook page level only
  • ✅Publish posts, stories, reels on the Page
  • ✅Manage comments, messages, DMs
  • ✅Add/remove other Page roles
  • ✅Create ads using the Page identity
  • ❌Cannot access the BM itself
  • ❌Cannot see ad account billing or data
💳
Ad Account Admin
ad account level only
  • ✅Create, edit, pause, delete campaigns
  • ✅Manage billing & payment methods
  • ✅Add/remove users from this ad account
  • ✅Full reporting & data access
  • ❌Cannot access the BM or other ad accounts
  • ❌Cannot touch the Facebook Page directly
🖊
Advertiser / Media Buyer
ad account — restricted
  • ✅Build and launch campaigns & ad sets
  • ✅Edit existing campaigns and creatives
  • ✅View all reporting and performance data
  • ❌Cannot change billing or payment method
  • ❌Cannot add or remove other users
  • ℹ️Best role for external freelancers / agencies
🤝
Partner (Agency BM)
connected via partner bm id
  • ✅Accesses only the specific assets you share
  • ✅Agency manages their own team internally
  • ✅You can revoke all access in one click
  • ❌Never inside your BM — connected externally
  • ❌Cannot see your other assets or users
  • ⚠️Never add agency staff directly into your BM
06— What Happens When Each Account Gets Hacked
This is the most critical section. The damage scales dramatically depending on which level that person had access to. A hacked BM Admin is catastrophic and potentially irreversible. Click each row to see the full blast radius.
📊 Analyst Account Hacked
View-only role · read access only
Low Risk▼
What attacker can see
Performance reports, campaign metrics, audience insights
What attacker can DO
Nothing. Analysts are read-only — no edits, no spending
Financial damage
None — cannot access billing or spend budget
Recovery difficulty
Very easy — remove user from asset, done
Main risk: Competitor intelligence — strategy, best-performing ads, and audience segments. Remove access immediately.
✏️ Page Editor / Moderator Hacked
Page content & comments only
Low–Med▼
What attacker can do
Post content on your Page, reply to comments, manage messages
Brand damage
High — can post offensive/false content before you notice
Financial damage
None — no billing or ad account access
Recovery difficulty
Easy — remove role, delete rogue posts
Main risk: Reputational damage. Remove role, delete content, post a correction if needed. No financial exposure.
🖊 Advertiser / Media Buyer Hacked
Can create campaigns & spend budget
Medium▼
What attacker can do
Create campaigns, change targeting, edit creatives, boost spend
Financial damage
Can drain ad budget but CANNOT change billing or increase credit limits
Data exposure
Full campaign data, audiences, pixel events, conversions
Recovery difficulty
Moderate — remove user, pause campaigns, review spend
Main risk: Budget drain. Set strict daily budget caps — spending stops at the cap regardless of what an advertiser does.
💳 Ad Account Admin Hacked
Full billing + user control on ad account
High Risk▼
What attacker can do
Everything an advertiser can, PLUS change billing method and modify users
Financial damage
Can swap payment method to their card or run up charges on yours
Access damage
Can remove other admins from the ad account
Recovery difficulty
Hard — need a BM Admin to intervene and reverse changes
Key insight: Never give Ad Account Admin to external parties. Advertiser role is enough for agencies to run campaigns without touching billing.
📄 Facebook Page Admin Hacked
Manages roles & brand identity
High Risk▼
What attacker can do
Post as your brand, change name/profile/cover, add their own admins, remove your access
Brand damage
Severe — can fully impersonate or alter your brand identity
Access damage
Can remove other Page Admins. BM Admin overrides if Page is BM-owned.
Recovery difficulty
Moderate if in BM — BM Admin can fix. Permanent lock-out if personally owned.
Critical lesson: Always own your Page inside a Business Manager. On a personal page with no BM, you could be permanently locked out.
🚨 Business Manager Admin Hacked
Worst possible scenario — controls everything
☠️ CRITICAL▼
Immediate access
Every ad account, every page, every pixel, every catalog — all of it
Financial damage
Drain all ad budgets, add payment methods, approve large charges
Lock-out risk
Can remove ALL other admins — permanently lock you out of your own BM
Data theft
Full customer audiences, pixel events, CRM lists, revenue conversion data
Recovery difficulty
Extremely hard. Meta support required. Days or weeks. Some assets lost permanently.
Cascade damage
Can revoke all agency access, breaking every active campaign immediately
PROTECTION: (1) Always have 2+ BM Admins. (2) Hardware security key — not SMS 2FA. (3) Company email with enforced 2FA. (4) Audit BM admins every quarter.
07— With Business Manager vs Without
❌ Without Business Manager
  • 💀If Page creator's account is hacked or deleted, the Page may be gone forever
  • 💀No central way to remove a rogue person's access across all assets at once
  • 💀Agency staff added as personal admins — chase each one when they leave
  • 💀Ad accounts tied to personal accounts — data can leave with the person
  • 💀No audit trail of who has access to what
  • 💀Pixel owned personally — leaving employee could lose pixel history
✅ With Business Manager
  • 🛡️BM owns assets — removing a user removes all their access instantly
  • 🛡️Agency gets Partner access — one click revokes their entire team
  • 🛡️Full audit trail of every person and which assets they can access
  • 🛡️System users for API — no real person's account tied to integrations
  • 🛡️Pixel, catalog, and data survive staff turnover entirely
  • 🛡️Multiple admins as backup — single point of failure eliminated
08— Golden Rules of Meta Access Security
01

Always have 2 BM Admins

If your only BM Admin loses access — hacked, deleted, left the company — you may be permanently locked out. Always maintain a backup admin on a separate secure account.

02

Create everything inside BM

Pages, pixels, ad accounts, catalogs — all created inside the BM, never personal accounts. Assets belong to the business, not the individual.

03

Never give more access than needed

A media buyer needs Advertiser, not Admin. An agency needs Partner access, not an employee seat. Match the role to the minimum required task.

04

Agencies via Partner BM only

Never add agency staff as direct users in your BM. Connect via their Business Manager ID. When the relationship ends, one click removes their entire team instantly.

05

Hardware 2FA for BM Admins

SMS 2FA can be SIM-swapped. BM Admin accounts must use an authenticator app or hardware security key (YubiKey). Non-negotiable for high-spend accounts.

06

Audit access quarterly

Go to Business Settings → People every 3 months. Remove anyone who no longer needs access. Ex-employees with dormant access are a silent, ongoing security risk.
